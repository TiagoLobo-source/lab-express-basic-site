José Afonso

José Afonso, also known as Zeca Afonso, was a highly influential Portuguese musician, singer, and songwriter. He was born on August 2, 1929, in Aveiro, Portugal, and he passed away on February 23, 1987, in Setúbal, Portugal. Zeca Afonso was a prominent figure in the Portuguese music scene, and he played a crucial role in the development of the popular music genre known as "Folk Revival" in Portugal.

He was a multi-talented artist who combined his passion for music with his strong political and social beliefs. Zeca Afonso's music was deeply rooted in the traditional folk music of Portugal, and he used it as a means to express his opposition to the fascist regime that ruled Portugal under António de Oliveira Salazar's Estado Novo regime.

During his lifetime, Zeca Afonso composed and performed many powerful and politically charged songs that became anthems for the resistance against the dictatorship. One of his most famous songs is "Grândola, Vila Morena," which played a significant role during the Carnation Revolution on April 25, 1974. This peaceful revolution led to the overthrow of the dictatorship and the establishment of a democratic government in Portugal.

Zeca Afonso's music continues to inspire and influence generations of Portuguese artists, and he remains a symbol of artistic and political expression in the country. His legacy endures through his songs, which are celebrated not only for their musical brilliance but also for their social and historical significance in Portugal's recent past.
